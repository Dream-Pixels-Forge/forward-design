## 1. Identity & Voice

### Core Identity
Rodrigue Ilunga — known professionally as **Forward Design** — is a **visual communicator** first, and an artist second. He is not someone who makes things "look pretty." He uses image-making as a form of **storytelling, idea expression, and provocation**. His work lives at the intersection of art and social purpose.

- **Social impact** — art should serve community, not just gallery
- **Authenticity** — "visuels forts, authentiques et porteurs de message"
- **Light as creative material** — "mise en scène de lumière"

### What's Implied But Not Said

- He is **multidisciplinary** (photography + graphic design) — the site must accommodate both without siloing them awkwardly
- He is **Kinshasa-based** — the cultural context of the DRC should feel present, not erased by a generic Western portfolio template

## 5. Design Direction

### 5.1 — Visual Style
**Cinematic editorial portfolio** with gallery-level sophistication and African warmth.

Think:
- **Awwwards-level immersive scrolling** — large imagery, smooth transitions, purposeful motion
- **Editorial magazine layout** — asymmetric grids, generous whitespace, typographic hierarchy
- **Dark gallery aesthetic** — dark or near-black backgrounds that make images breathe and pop
- **Warm accent colors** — amber, gold, or burnt orange (evoking Kinshasa light, not cold corporate blue)
- **Minimal UI chrome** — the interface gets out of the way; the work is the interface

### 5.2 — Typography
| Role | Recommendation | Rationale |
|------|---------------|-----------|
| **Headings / Display** | A bold, expressive serif (e.g., Playfair Display, Canela, or a custom display font) | Matches the poetic, artistic voice |
| **Body / UI** | A clean, highly legible sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Satoshi, or Suisse) | Ensures readability and modern feel |
| **Accent / Taglines** | Optionally, a mono-spaced or geometric font for "Forward Design" branding | Reinforces the graphic design credential |

### 5.3 — Color Palette
| Role | Color | Hex |
|------|-------|-----|
| **Background** | Deep charcoal / near-black | `#0a0a0a` to `#1a1a1a` |
| **Primary text** | Warm white | `#f5f0eb` |
| **Accent** | Amber / gold | `#d4a843` or `#c8956c` |
| **Secondary accent** | Warm gray (for metadata, captions) | `#6b6b6b` |
| **Highlight / CTA** | Burnt orange or copper | `#c67a3c` |

**Why these colors:** Dark backgrounds honor the gallery aesthetic. Warm accents reflect Kinshasa's golden hour light. The palette is restrained enough to let photography dominate but distinctive enough to feel designed.

### 5.4 — Layout Philosophy
- **Image-first, always.** Typography and UI exist to frame and contextualize images, never compete with them.
- **Asymmetric grids.** Break the center-column cliché. Use offset compositions that feel editorial and intentional.
- **Full-bleed imagery.** Let photographs stretch edge to edge. No timid borders.
- **Scroll as narrative.** Use scrollytelling techniques — as the user scrolls, images reveal, text fades in, the story unfolds. This mirrors his "mise en scène" approach.
- **White (dark) space is sacred.** Give every image room to breathe. Crowding dilutes impact.

### 5.5 — Motion & Interaction
| Element | Behavior |
|---------|----------|
| **Hero** | Cinematic sequence — the existing `hero_sequences/` frames (24 WebP images) should animate as an intro loop or scroll-driven reveal |
| **Scroll** | Smooth, inertia-based scrolling (Lenis or equivalent) with parallax layers |
| **Image hover** | Subtle scale or reveal — metadata (title, context) appears on hover/tap |
| **Page transitions** | Crossfade or slide — no hard cuts |
| **Text reveals** | Words or lines fade/slide in on scroll — staggered, not all at once |
| **Cursor** | Optionally, a custom cursor (dot or crosshair) for gallery feel |

### 5.6 — What to Avoid
- ❌ Generic portfolio templates (squarespace-style grids)
- ❌ Bright white backgrounds (feels clinical, not gallery)
- ❌ Excessive UI chrome (nav bars, sidebars, widgets competing with work)
- ❌ Stock photography or placeholder imagery (everything must be his actual work)
- ❌ Social media feeds embedded prominently (distracts from the curated experience)
- ❌ "Africa as aesthetic" clichés (no tribal patterns as backgrounds, no safari imagery)
- ❌ Over-animation (motion should serve the story, not be the story)

## 8. Technical Recommendations

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Framework** | Next.js or Astro for performance + SEO |
| **Image format** | WebP (already in use) with AVIF fallback |
| **Image loading** | Lazy-load below fold, preload hero sequence |
| **Hero animation** | Frame-by-frame scroll-linked or timed loop (GSAP or Framer Motion) |
| **Smooth scroll** | Lenis Smooth Scroll or Locomotive Scroll |
| **CMS** | Sanity, Contentful, or MDX for easy project updates |
| **Performance** | Aim for <2s LCP, <100KB CSS, image optimization pipeline |
| **Accessibility** | Alt text on all images, keyboard navigation, sufficient contrast |
| **SEO** | Open Graph images for social sharing, structured data for Person/CreativeWork |
| **Analytics** | Plausible or Fathom (privacy-respecting, lightweight) |
